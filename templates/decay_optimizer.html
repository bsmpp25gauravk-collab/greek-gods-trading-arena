📚Core Concepts

⏳

Why Theta is Non-Linear

Options lose time value slowly early in their life, then accelerate dramatically near expiry. The decay is proportional to the square root of time — not linear.

Time Value ≈ Initial TV × √(T/T₀)
Final 4 days = same decay as first 12 days
🎯

The 4–5 DTE Sweet Spot

Entering 4–5 days before expiry captures maximum theta acceleration while keeping gamma manageable. You have time to adjust if the market moves against you.

Thu/Fri entry → Wed expiry: OPTIMAL
Mon entry → Wed expiry: OPTIMAL
Tue entry → Wed expiry: AGGRESSIVE
📅

India Weekly Expiry Calendar

India has three overlapping weekly expiry cycles. Staggering positions across all three reduces concentration risk and provides continuous theta collection opportunities.

Tuesday: Finnifty expires
Wednesday: Bank Nifty expires
Thursday: Nifty 50 expires
🌊

Weekend Decay Advantage

Options decay on calendar days, not just trading days. Entering Friday captures 3 days of decay (Fri → Mon) while the market is closed. Premium sellers love Friday entries.

Friday close → Monday open:
~3 calendar days of theta collected
Risk: weekend gap events
⚡

Gamma Explosion in Final Days

Near expiry, gamma becomes extreme. A 1% move in Bank Nifty at 1 DTE can push an ATM option's delta from 0.50 to 0.75 — a 50% change in directional exposure.

1 DTE example (BN ATM Call):
Delta: 0.50 → 0.75 on 1% move
Exit BEFORE this happens
🏆

The 50% Profit Rule

Closing at 50% of max profit is mathematically optimal for premium sellers. It locks in the majority of theta collected while drastically reducing time-in-trade and gamma exposure.
