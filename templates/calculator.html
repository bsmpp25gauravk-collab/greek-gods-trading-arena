⚡ Pricing Models

Options Calculator

Three models — Black-Scholes-Merton, Binomial (CRR), and Put-Call Parity. Rate, IV, and Dividend as percentages.

Black-Scholes-Merton assumes log-normal price distribution, constant volatility and rates, and continuous dividend yield. Gives closed-form prices and exact Greeks (Δ, Γ, Θ, ν, ρ). Best for European-style index options.
Binomial (CRR) builds a 200-step recombining price tree. Numerically equivalent to BSM in the limit, but flexible enough for American-style early exercise. Greeks are computed via finite differences on the tree.
Put-Call Parity: C − P = Se−qT − Ke−rT. Enter a known Call or Put market price — get the implied fair value of the other side, plus an arbitrage gap check.
