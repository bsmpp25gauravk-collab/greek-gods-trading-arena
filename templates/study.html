Δ

DELTA — The God of Direction

How much does my option move when the stock moves?

What is Delta?

Delta (Δ) tells you how much your option's price changes when the underlying asset moves by ₹1. It is the most important Greek for day-to-day trading.

MATHEMATICAL DEFINITION
Δ = ∂V / ∂S
Where V = Option Price, S = Stock/Index Price
Delta (Call) = N(d₁)
Delta (Put) = N(d₁) – 1

Key Ranges

Deep OTM Call
≈ 0.05
ATM Call
≈ 0.50
Deep ITM Call
≈ 0.95
ATM Put
≈ -0.50
Deep ITM Put
≈ -0.95

Practical Uses

🎯 Hedge Ratio

Delta = 0.50 means to hedge 1 option lot, you need to buy/sell 0.50 × lot_size shares of the underlying.

🎲 Probability Proxy

An ATM call with delta 0.50 has roughly a 50% chance of expiring in-the-money. OTM call with delta 0.20 → ~20% chance.

📊 Portfolio Direction

Portfolio Delta = sum of all position deltas. A delta-neutral portfolio has ≈ zero directional risk.

⚡ Quick Facts

Call range0 to +1
Put range-1 to 0
ATM value≈ ±0.50
Unit₹ per ₹1 move

🇮🇳 NSE Example

Nifty at 23,741. You buy 1 lot (65 units) of the 23,750 CE (ATM call).

Delta ≈ 0.51.

If Nifty rises ₹100:
Option gains ≈ ₹51 per unit × 65 = ₹3,315 profit.

Dollar Delta:
0.51 × 23,741 × 65 = ₹7,87,735 equivalent exposure.

⚠️ Key Risk

Delta changes constantly as the stock moves. A position that is delta-neutral today becomes directional after any move — this is Gamma risk.

Γ

GAMMA — The God of Acceleration

How fast is my Delta changing?

What is Gamma?

Gamma (Γ) is the rate of change of Delta. If Delta tells you speed, Gamma tells you acceleration. High Gamma means your Delta (and therefore your P&L) can swing violently with small moves.

MATHEMATICAL DEFINITION
Γ = ∂²V / ∂S² = ∂Δ / ∂S
Γ = N'(d₁) / (S × σ × √T)
Where N'(d₁) = standard normal PDF at d₁

Why Gamma Matters

🚀 Convexity (Long Gamma)

Long options have positive gamma. This means you benefit more from big moves than the delta predicts — you win from both large up and large down moves.

💥 Short Gamma Risk

Option sellers have negative gamma. A 5% gap move can turn a small delta into a huge loss because delta changed so fast. GameStop 2021 was a gamma squeeze.

📅 Pin Risk at Expiry

ATM options near expiry have maximum gamma. A tiny Nifty move from 23,740 to 23,760 on expiry day can flip delta from 0.1 to 0.9 — massive delta change.

📰 Case Study: GameStop Gamma Squeeze (Jan 2021)

GME stock rose from $20 to $483 in weeks. Short-sellers had sold call options — giving them negative gamma exposure. As GME rose, their delta hedge required buying more and more shares. This buying drove the price higher, which forced more delta hedging, creating a self-reinforcing feedback loop.

Key lesson: Never carry large negative gamma near expiry without understanding your worst-case delta exposure.

⚡ Quick Facts

Long options+Gamma
Short options-Gamma
Highest atATM, near expiry
Lowest atDeep ITM/OTM

🇮🇳 NSE Example

Nifty at 23,741. ATM call gamma ≈ 0.00064.

If Nifty moves +100 points:

New Delta ≈ Old Delta + Gamma × 100
= 0.51 + 0.00064 × 100
= 0.574

On expiry day, gamma can be 10× higher — tiny moves cause huge delta swings.

⚠️ Key Risk

Being short gamma near expiry is extremely dangerous. Bank Nifty weekly options on Wednesday mornings can have gamma 20× the normal level.

Θ

THETA — The God of Time

How much value does my option lose each day?

What is Theta?

Theta (Θ) measures time decay — how much your option's price falls with every passing day, holding everything else constant. For long option buyers, theta is a silent enemy. For option sellers, it is a daily income stream.

MATHEMATICAL DEFINITION
Θ = ∂V / ∂t
Θ (Call) = –[S·N'(d₁)·σ / (2√T)] – r·K·e–rT·N(d₂)
Theta is typically expressed as value lost per day (divide annual theta by 365)

Time Decay Accelerates

Theta is not linear — it accelerates as expiry approaches. An option with 30 days to expiry decays slowly. In the final 7 days, decay speeds up dramatically. On expiry day itself, time value evaporates completely.

30 DTE — Slow decay
14 DTE — Moderate decay
7 DTE — Fast decay
1 DTE — Maximum decay
30 days  →  14 days  →  7 days  →  Expiry day
💰 Income Strategy

Short strangles, iron condors, and covered calls all collect Theta. Sellers earn the decay every day without needing the market to move.

⏳ Holding Period Risk

Long call buyers must predict direction AND timing. Even a correct directional call loses money if the stock moves too slowly — Theta eats the premium.

⚡ Quick Facts

Long optionsNegative Θ
Short optionsPositive Θ
Highest atATM, near expiry
Theta/dayAnnual Θ ÷ 365

🇮🇳 Bank Nifty Example

Bank Nifty weekly ATM option (Wednesday expiry). On Monday with 3 days to go, theta ≈ –₹25/day per lot.

By Tuesday: theta ≈ –₹50/day
By Wednesday AM: theta ≈ –₹120/day

Option sellers earn this. Buyers bleed it — even if Bank Nifty stays flat.

⚠️ Key Risk

Theta sellers are short gamma. The theta income can be wiped out instantly by a large gap move. Always know your gamma exposure when selling premium.

ν

VEGA — The God of Volatility

How much does my option move when Implied Volatility changes?

What is Vega?

Vega (ν) measures how much your option's price changes when Implied Volatility (IV) moves by 1%. Vega is unique — it affects your P&L even when the underlying doesn't move at all.

Note: Vega is not actually a Greek letter — but it is universally used in options trading.

MATHEMATICAL DEFINITION
ν = ∂V / ∂σ
ν = S × √T × N'(d₁)
Divide by 100 to get the value change per 1% IV move
📈 IV Expansion (Pre-Earnings)

Before a results announcement or RBI policy, IV rises. Long options gain value even if the stock doesn't move — purely from vega.

📉 IV Crush (Post-Event)

After results, IV collapses. Even if you guessed the direction right, your long option can lose money because IV dropped 20 points — vega destroyed the gain.

📰 Case Study: XIV Volatility Collapse (Feb 2018 — "Volmageddon")

On Feb 5, 2018, the VIX doubled from 17 to 37 in a single day. The XIV ETN (short volatility product) collapsed from $99 to under $5 overnight — a 96% loss — and was terminated.

What happened in Greek terms: XIV was massively short vega. Each 1% rise in VIX caused escalating losses. The product's rebalancing mechanics amplified the losses in a self-reinforcing spiral.

Key lesson: Vega limits must be stress-tested for extreme scenarios. Volatility can go far beyond "normal" levels.

⚡ Quick Facts

Long options+Vega
Short options-Vega
Highest atATM, long-dated
Per 1% IV move₹Vega change

🇮🇳 NSE Earnings Example

Infosys results tomorrow. IV is at 35%. You buy an ATM call with Vega = 11.

Pre-results, IV rises to 45% (+10 vol points).

Gain from IV alone:
10 × 11 = ₹110 per unit
× 65 lots = ₹7,150 profit

After results, IV crashes to 20% — IV crush wipes out all gains.

⚠️ Key Risk

Buying options before events at elevated IV is dangerous — you pay high premium, and if the stock moves less than expected, IV collapses and you lose even if direction was right.

ρ

RHO — The God of Interest Rates

How does my option price change when interest rates change?

What is Rho?

Rho (ρ) measures how much your option price changes when the risk-free interest rate changes by 1%. Of all the Greeks, Rho has the smallest day-to-day impact for short-dated options — but it becomes significant for LEAPS (long-dated options).

MATHEMATICAL DEFINITION
ρ = ∂V / ∂r
ρ (Call) = K × T × e–rT × N(d₂)
ρ (Put) = –K × T × e–rT × N(–d₂)
📈 Call Options

Positive Rho — call options benefit when rates rise. Higher rates make the present value of the strike cheaper, making calls more valuable.

📉 Put Options

Negative Rho — put options are hurt when rates rise. The put's present value advantage decreases as rates go up.

📅 LEAPS (Long-Dated)

For options with 1+ year to expiry, Rho can be as important as Vega. A 1% RBI rate change can meaningfully impact long-dated option values.

⚡ Quick Facts

CallsPositive ρ
PutsNegative ρ
ImpactLow for weekly
Important forLEAPS (1yr+)

🇮🇳 RBI Example

Current RBI repo rate: 5.28%.
Nifty 3-month ATM call, Rho ≈ 0.15.

If RBI hikes rate by 50 bps (0.50%):

Call gains ≈ 0.15 × 0.50 = ₹0.075 per unit

Small for weekly options. But for a 12-month call, Rho ≈ 5.0 — that's a ₹2.50/unit move.

PORTFOLIO GREEKS — Managing the Full Picture

Individual Greeks add up — and that sum is what really matters.

How Portfolio Greeks Work

When you hold multiple options positions, you don't look at Greeks one position at a time. You aggregate them to see your total risk exposure.

AGGREGATION FORMULAS
Portfolio Delta = Σ (Position Size × Individual Delta)
Portfolio Gamma = Σ (Position Size × Individual Gamma)
Portfolio Theta = Σ (Position Size × Individual Theta)
Portfolio Vega = Σ (Position Size × Individual Vega)
Position Size = Number of contracts × Lot Size

Dollar Greeks — Seeing Risk in Rupee Terms

Dollar Delta = Delta × Underlying Price × Lot Size × Contracts
Dollar Gamma = Gamma × Underlying Price² × Lot Size × 0.01
Dollar Theta = Theta × Lot Size × Contracts
Dollar Greeks show your risk in actual money terms, not abstract Greek values

Risk Limit Framework

Professional traders define hard limits for each Greek. Your dashboard alerts you when you approach these limits.

GreekNormal ZoneCaution ZoneBreachAction if Breached
Portfolio Delta 0 – 70% of limit 70–90% >100 Sell futures / Buy opposite options
Portfolio Gamma 0 – 70% of limit 70–90% >20 Reduce short near-ATM positions
Portfolio Theta Positive –₹500/day <–₹1,000/day Rebalance premium exposure
Portfolio Vega 0 – 70% of limit 70–90% >500 Cut long vol exposure before event
