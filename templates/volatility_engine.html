📚Conceptual Foundation

📈

What is IV Rank?

IV Rank contextualises current implied volatility against its 52-week range. It answers: where does today's IV sit between the annual high and low?

IV Rank = (Current IV − 52wk Low)
            ÷ (52wk High − 52wk Low) × 100
📊

What is IV Percentile?

IV Percentile shows what percentage of days over the past year had lower IV than today. It considers the full distribution, making it more robust than IV Rank.

IV Pctl = (Days with IV < Current IV)
            ÷ Total Days × 100
🔄

Why IV Reverts to Mean

Volatility exhibits strong mean-reverting behaviour. Extremes are statistically unlikely to persist — high IV tends to fall, low IV tends to rise. Mean reversion half-life is approximately 2–4 weeks, giving options traders a systematic edge.

⚖️

IV vs HV Premium

When IV exceeds HV, options are expensive (volatility premium exists — favour selling). When IV is below HV, options are cheap (discount — favour buying). On average, IV exceeds HV by 2–3 percentage points — the systematic edge for premium sellers.

🇮🇳

India VIX Context

India VIX measures expected 30-day Nifty 50 volatility. Normal range: 10–20. Spikes before elections, RBI policy, and budgets create premium-selling opportunities post-event as IV collapses sharply.

Low: <12 · Normal: 12–20
Elevated: 20–25 · Crisis: >35
🎯

IV Rank vs Percentile — Which to Use?

IV Rank is sensitive to outliers (one crisis spike can suppress readings for a year). IV Percentile considers the full distribution. Best practice: use both together for confirmation — when both agree, the signal is stronger.
