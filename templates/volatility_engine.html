📚 Conceptual Foundation

IV Rank contextualises current implied volatility against its 52-week range. It answers: where does today's IV sit between the annual high and low?

IV Rank = (Current IV − 52wk Low) / (52wk High − 52wk Low) × 100

Key caveat: IV Rank is sensitive to outliers. One crisis spike can suppress readings for an entire year. Always confirm with IV Percentile.

IV Percentile shows what percentage of days over the past year had lower IV than today. It considers the full distribution, making it more robust than IV Rank.

IV Percentile = (Days with IV < Current IV) / Total Days × 100

Volatility exhibits strong mean-reverting behaviour. Extreme high or low readings are statistically unlikely to persist. High IV tends to fall, low IV tends to rise. The mean reversion half-life is approximately 2–4 weeks — giving premium sellers a systematic edge. On average, IV exceeds HV by 2–3 percentage points (the "volatility risk premium").

India VIX measures expected 30-day Nifty 50 volatility. It spikes sharply before elections, RBI policy, and Union Budget announcements, then collapses post-event — creating ideal premium-selling setups.
